Everything you need to know about Signum.

The basics
What is Signum?
Signum is an AI-powered website trust intelligence platform. Paste any domain and get a risk score (0–100), a verdict (Safe / Caution / Danger), and a plain-English analysis of what we found — in seconds.
What does Signum check?
We run 15+ checks in parallel: VirusTotal, Google Safe Browsing, IPQS, AbuseIPDB, SSL certificate analysis, WHOIS and domain age, DNS records, Wayback Machine history, Trustpilot, Shodan, OTX AlienVault, business registries, brand similarity detection, and live homepage content analysis. The results are synthesised by AI into a single verdict.
How accurate is Signum?
Very accurate for known threats and freshly-registered suspicious domains. No automated tool is 100% definitive — always apply your own judgement alongside the verdict. If something feels wrong, trust that instinct regardless of the score.
How long does a scan take?
Most scans complete in 5–10 seconds. Results are cached for 6 hours, so repeat scans of the same domain are near-instant.

Chrome extension
What does the Chrome extension do?
The Signum Chrome extension checks every site you visit in real time. If you land on a high-risk domain, it shows a warning banner before you interact with the page.
Is the extension free?
Yes, the extension is free to install and use.
Does the extension track my browsing?
No. The extension only checks the domain of the current page — it does not log your browsing history or send personal data anywhere. See our privacy policy for full details.

Email header analysis
What is the email header analyser?
Paste the raw headers from any suspicious email and Signum checks SPF, DKIM, DMARC, routing hops, sending IPs, and subject keywords — then gives you a plain-English verdict on whether the email is legitimate or a phishing attempt.
How do I get the raw headers from an email?
In Gmail: open the email, click the three dots menu, select "Show original". In Outlook: open the email, go to File → Properties, copy the "Internet headers" section.
